Visit

As a UNESCO World Heritage site, Jantar Mantar is not only an architectural wonder but also a priceless repository of scientific knowledge. The conservation of this monument involves a multi-faceted approach, combining modern restoration techniques with traditional craftsmanship. The preservation efforts are crucial to maintaining the structural integrity of the instruments while ensuring they remain functional for future generations of scholars and astronomers.

Rajasthan Tourism:

https://www.tourism.rajasthan.gov.in/jantar-mantar.html

Visiting Hours

Jantar Mantar is open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM, seven days a week.

Ticket Information

Tickets can be purchased at the entrance or online. For international visitors, the ticket price is ₹200, while for Indian visitors, the price is ₹50. A guided tour or an audio guide is highly recommended to fully understand the scientific and historical significance of the site; these arrangements may be made on site. Tickets may be booked in advance at the website of the Rajasthan Tourism Department at

https://obms-tourist.rajasthan.gov.in/place-details/Jantar-Mantar

Getting Here

Located in the heart of Jaipur, Jantar Mantar is easily accessible by car, taxi, or public transport. The monument is close to other attractions such as the City Palace and Hawa Mahal, making it part of a perfect day of exploration.

Experience More

Don’t miss the opportunity to explore the full array of astronomical instruments and learn about the cosmic wonders that captivated the minds of ancient scholars.
