COSMIC CHRONICLES INDIA

India’s Gaganyaan and Chandrayaan missions have reaffirmed India’s position as a space powerhouse. There are many other milestones in India’s space journey -

1963★

India's space journey began at TERLS in Thumba, near Thiruvananthapuram, close to the magnetic equator.

First sounding rocket, the Nike-Apache, launches from TERLS, procured from the USA.

1967★

Satellite Telecommunication Earth Station set up at Ahmedabad.

First indigenous sounding rocket, RH-75, launches

1975★

Aryabhata, first Indian satellite launches from the former Soviet Union.

1976★

ISRO and NASA develop space communication system for TV broadcasting.

1981★

Launch of APPLE, an experimental geo-stationary communication satellite. Becomes the frontrunner for future communication satellites.

1980★

In 1980, the second experimental launch of SLV-3 when Rohini was successfully placed in orbit.

1979★

Bhaskara-I, first experimental satellite for Earth observations.

First experimental launch of SLV-3 with Rohini Technology Payload on board.

1977★

STEP, a joint project of ISRO and P&T for telecommunication experiments.

1982★

Launch of INSAT-IA for the purposes of communication, broadcasting and meteorology.

1984★

First Indo-Soviet manned space mission launches. Rakesh Sharma, first Indian to go into space.

1987★

First developmental launch of ASLV, which supports a larger payload than the SLV-3 Planned as a low-cost option

1988★

First operational Indian Remote Sensing Satellite, IRS-1A.

2007★

INSAT-4 CR weighing 2130kg, becomes India's heaviest satellite launch onboard GSLV-Fo4.

2001★

First developmental launch of GSLV-DI with GSAT-1 on board takes off from Sriharikota, Made for Geosynchronous communication satellites.

1999★

PSLV starts carrying foreign payloads along with ISRO's satellite Oceansat.

1994★

Second developmental launch of PSLV with IRS-P2. Satellite successfully placed in polar Sun-synchronous orbit. Becomes a favoured carrier for satellites of various countries due to its reliability and cost-eficiency.

2008★

PSLV-C launches Chandrayaan-I from Sriharikota. Operational for 312 days till 28 August 2009.

2013★

PSLV C-25 launches Mars orbiter Mission spacecraft from Sriharikota.

2014★

India becomes the fourth space agency to reach Mars.

2016★

India tests its first indigenous Reusable Launch Vehicle (RLV)

2020★

India's telecommunication satellite GSAT-30 is launched into a GTO from French Guiana.

PSLV successfuly launches EOS-01 and nine customer satellites from SDSC

2019★

GSLV MkIII-Mi rocket, carrying Chandrayaan-2 launches from SDSC

2018★

Indian Cabinet approves Indian human spaceflight initiative Gaganyaan.

PSLV-C46 successfully launches RISAT-2B satellite from SDSC

2017★

GSAT-17 satellite Launch.

PSLV-C38/Cartosat-2 Series Satellite Mission launches from SDSC.

2021★

EOS-03, an Earth observation satellite is launched by GSLV-FLO from SDSC.

2022★

In its second operational flight, LVM3 launch vehicle placed 36 One Web satellites into their intended orbits.

2023★

PSLV-C54 has successfully launched EOS-06 satellite along with eight nano satellites into two different SSPOs.

2024★

LVM3-M4 vehicle successfully launched Chandrayaan-3, headed for the Moon, from SDSC. It soft-landed successfully

The launch of Aditya-Li by PSLV-C57 is accomplished successfully.
